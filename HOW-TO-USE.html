📖 How to use the app — step by step

A simple guide, from signing in to uploading your receipts. All from your phone, no tech skills needed 🙂

👉 https://ai-finance-insights.netlify.app
🌐 Tip: the app opens in English automatically on an English phone. If you ever see Spanish, just tap the ES / EN switch at the top to change it.

1Open the app

  1. Go to the link above on your phone (Safari or Chrome).
  2. (Optional) Tap Share → "Add to Home Screen" and you get an icon like any other app.

2Create your account

  1. Tap Sign up / Create account.
  2. Enter your email and a password (at least 6 characters).
  3. You're in right away — no email confirmation needed.
  4. Next time: open the same link and enter your email + password under Sign in.
🔒 Your information is private: each person only sees their own data.

3Get to know the screen

  • At the top it greets you by name, with the "Ask the AI" chat.
  • Below: your Income, Spent, Saved, your week, your wallets, the dollar rate, your pension (AFP) and a calendar.
  • To switch sections (Overview, Analytics, Subscriptions, Budgets, Goals) use the dropdown menu under the greeting.
  • Switch ☀️ Light / 🌙 Dark, change the ES / EN language, and set your name or photo with the round button at the top.

4Add your transactions (3 ways)

A) Scan a Yape / Plin receipt 📷 (easiest)

  1. Scroll to "Scan Yape / Plin receipts".
  2. Tap "Upload receipts" and pick the screenshot of your receipt from your gallery.
  3. The app reads it automatically (amount and date). Review and save. You can upload several!

💡 Yape and Plin are Peru's mobile-payment apps. If you don't use them, just use option B or C.

B) Import a bank file (CSV) 📄

  1. Go to "Import transactions".
  2. Choose the currency (Soles or Dollars) and, if you like, where it's from (Yape, a bank, etc.).
  3. Upload the .csv file you downloaded from your online banking.

C) Add it by hand ✍️

  1. Under "Your transactions", tap "+ Add transaction" and fill it in.

5Categories are set automatically 🏷️

  • When you upload or scan, the AI categorizes them for you (food, transport, bills…).
  • Anything left as "Other"? Tap "Re-categorize all".
  • One came out wrong? Tap that transaction → change it → check "Apply to similar transactions" so the app learns for next time.

6Track your pension (AFP) 🏦

  1. On the "AFP" card, tap "View →".
  2. Add your balance by hand, or scan your statement. (Before you add anything, you'll see a sample example.)

💡 AFP is Peru's private pension fund — handy if you have one; otherwise you can ignore this card.

7Convert dollars and soles 💱

Tap the "Exchange rate" bar, choose Official or Parallel, type an amount and use ⇄ to convert Soles ⇄ Dollars.

8Payment reminders 🔔

Under "Upcoming payments", note your regular bills (electricity, internet, rent…) with a name, amount and day. The app reminds you as they come up.

9Ask the AI 💬

At the top, type your question (e.g. "What did I spend most on this month?") or tap a suggestion. It answers using your own data — in English.

10See your analytics 📊

In the section menu choose "Analytics" for charts of your spending and trends.

11Sign out and back in

To leave: "Sign out" (top). To come back: open the link and sign in with your email and password — your data is still there.

⚠️ This is a beta — it's just getting started and may have a few rough edges. Your feedback is what helps most. Try it with your real spending for a few days and tell me: what did you like? what didn't work or looked off? what's missing?
